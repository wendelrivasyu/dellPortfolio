Library Management

LES-LPACS

The Limba Elementary School – Local Public Access Catalog (LES-LPAC) System is a database-driven application designed to digitize and streamline the cataloging of library resources, enabling students, teachers, and administrators to quickly search, retrieve, and manage books and references; by integrating user-friendly search functions, borrowing records, and inventory management, the system reduces manual errors, improves circulation efficiency, and enhances accessibility of educational materials, resulting in better resource utilization and stronger support for academic learning.

Result: Enhanced accessibility of library resources, reduced manual cataloging errors, and improved efficiency in book circulation — leading to better resource utilization and stronger support for academic learning.
Inventory Management

LNU : Supply Inventory Management System

The Supply Inventory Management System developed for the Supply Office of Leyte Normal University is a technology-based solution designed to replace inefficient manual processes with automated, real-time inventory tracking, reporting, and procurement management. Anchored on inventory management theories such as the Two-Bin System, Just-in-Time, and Vendor-Managed Inventory, the system integrates database-driven functions (CRUD operations, purchase order generation, and report automation) to streamline resource handling. Using the System Development Life Cycle (SDLC) and action research methodology, the project assessed current inefficiencies, designed user-friendly interfaces, and implemented features that address stockouts, overstocking, and inaccurate records.

Result: The system significantly improved transaction speed, accuracy, and transparency; 80% of supply office personnel reported higher satisfaction due to reduced manual workload, enhanced efficiency, and better decision-making, leading to lower operational costs and optimized resource availability across the university’s departments.

Published Research

A collection of my academic studies and research published in online journals and repositories.

Published: International Journal of Innovative Science and Research Technology • 2023

Leyte Normal University: Supply Inventory Management System

Abstract:- Business success in today's dynamic marketplace depends on effective supply inventory management. In this technologically advanced age, conventional manual methods are no longer sufficient. To improve accuracy, efficiency, and decision-making abilities, this study investigates the significance of technology adoption for efficient inventory management. It concludes several research studies and emphasizes the difficulties Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) in the Philippines face and their distinctive inventory management procedures. The supply office at Leyte Normal University is the focus of the proposed study, which aims to create an effective system for managing inventories. The study uses the action research methodology to design, implement, and evaluate a technology-based system that addresses current inefficiencies. The two-bin system, the just-in-time theory, and vendor-management inventory are examples of theoretical frameworks. Surveys using the Likert scale and open-ended questions will be used to gather data, and the results will show how the suggested method better satisfies user needs and increases efficiency. Advanced data analytics should be used, vendor collaboration should be encouraged, and customized reporting should be made possible. This study supports competitive enterprises, effective educational institutions, and improved inventory management methods.
